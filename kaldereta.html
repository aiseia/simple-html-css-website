How to Make a Delicious and Savory Chicken Kaldereta: A Step-by-Step Guide

By: AJ Evasco



About the Dish


Kaldereta is a mouthwatering and hearty Filipino stew that exemplifies the rich and diverse flavors of Filipino cuisine. This beloved dish typically features tender chunks of meat, such as beef, goat, or chicken, slow-cooked to perfection in a luscious tomato-based sauce. What sets Kaldereta apart is its vibrant blend of influences, showcasing the Philippines' historical ties to Spanish, Mexican, and indigenous culinary traditions. This fusion of flavors is further accentuated by the use of aromatic spices, including bay leaves, black peppercorns, and red chili peppers, which lend a delightful complexity to the dish.

To complement the savory base, Kaldereta is often embellished with an array of vegetables like potatoes, bell peppers, and green olives, adding both texture and a burst of color to the dish. Served piping hot and typically accompanied by steamed rice, Kaldereta is a warm and inviting comfort food that embodies the Filipino spirit of hospitality and celebration, making it a cherished centerpiece at family gatherings and special occasions across the archipelago. Whether you're savoring it in the bustling streets of Manila or recreating it in your own kitchen, Kaldereta is a flavorful journey into the heart of Filipino cooking, a testament to the nation's culinary artistry and its rich cultural tapestry.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds (about 1 kg) chicken, cut into serving pieces (you can use a whole chicken or your preferred chicken parts, like thighs, drumsticks, or breast)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (vegetable or canola oil)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1-2 potatoes, peeled and sliced into rounds or cubes
  • 1-2 carrots, peeled and sliced into rounds or cubes
  • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup liver spread (you can use liver spread in a can)
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (typically quick-melt cheese like Eden or processed cheese)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 red chili pepper (optional, for added heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce and vinegar. Place the chicken pieces in the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated.
  2. Allow them to marinate for at least 15-30 minutes. This step is optional but adds flavor to the chicken.
  3. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  4. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pot.
  5. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
  6. If you marinated the chicken, remove it from the marinade and add it to the pot. Sauté the chicken until it turns lightly browned on all sides.
  7. Add the sliced red and green bell peppers, potatoes, carrots, and green peas to the pot.
  8. Stir and cook for a few minutes to slightly soften the vegetables.
  9. Add the tomato sauce to the pot. Stir to combine it with the chicken and vegetables.
  10. Stir in the liver spread and grated cheese. These ingredients will add richness and creaminess to the sauce. Continue stirring until they are fully incorporated.
  11. Add the bay leaves and, if you like it spicy, the red chili pepper.
  12. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the Chicken Kaldereta simmer for about 20-30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  13. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
  14. Season the dish with salt and pepper to taste. You can also adjust the thickness of the sauce by adding more tomato sauce if desired.
  15. Remove the bay leaves and chili pepper (if used). Garnish the Chicken Kaldereta with chopped fresh parsley or cilantro leaves if desired. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Enjoy your homemade Chicken Kaldereta, a flavorful Filipino classic that's perfect for gatherings or family dinners!
